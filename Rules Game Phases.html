Game Phases

Mechanized is a game about building a huge battle robot, called a "Mech" and then having fun blowing up your friend's Mechs and getting yours blown up, too!

The gameplay follows the list of phases to the right. Each phase has its own rules. It may seem complex, but by-in-large, the pattern is that we get resources, then we use resources to configure our robots, then we fight with our robots to see who gets the best of the next round of resources. The winner is basically the player who wins the most robot fights.

Setup

There are three steps to getting your mech set up at the begining of the game. These steps can be taken in any order.

Draw Starting Cards

When the game starts, you will be dealt a hand of 6 cards. Don't show your cards to your opponents. From this initial set, pick three cards to start in your hand. Cards do very useful things for you, such as allow your mech to move faster or shoot more accurately during combat, or purchase better weapons or systems during the build phase. The cards that you start with can change the strategy that you hope to try.

Picking A Mech Chassis

Before you can build the mech that you'll take into combat, you'll need to pick one of the available battleframes to build it on. Check out the Chassis handout and choose the one that fits your strategy. The chassis that you pick will be the one that you stick with through the whole game, so choose carefully.

New players are encouraged to pick a chassis that are "all around-ers," like Artemis or Loki, since they are slightly easier to play.

Picking a Pilot Class

At the start of the game you get to choose one pilot class, which gives your character some helpful bonus. The class you select will be yours for the duration of the game; it cannot be changed mid-game. More than one player can choose the same class, but no player can choose more than one class.

Rounds

There are three steps that everyone goes through each round. The steps are the same for every round. The steps must be done in order, and everyone does each step at the same time.

Building

Everyone gets credits at the beginning of the Build phase, and everyone makes new purchases, sells old equipment, and modifies what equipment their mech has equipped at the same time during the build phase. Do not let your opponents know what modifications are being made to your mech during the build phase; many strategies have counters to them, and your opponent could use your purchasing decisions against you.

For information on how to buy, sell, and build a mech, see the "Building Rules" page.

Combat

Turn Order: Decide what order players take their turn in whatever way you see fit. Tradition dictates that players roll a 12-sided dice, with the highest roll going first, but feel free to spit in the face of tradition.

See the Combat Rules page for details on moving, shooting, idling, and retreating.

Out of the fight: During Combat, players will get destroyed or will retreat from the combat area. Keep track of the order that players are knocked out of the fight. The player that is the last player standing won; they came in first place. The last player that was destroyed or retreated came in second place, and so on. You gain tiebreaker points equal to the place you took. So the person who came in first gets one victory point, the person who came in 4th gets 4 tiebreaker points, etc. See the "declaring a winner" section below.

Even if a player's robot with all of their weapons and subsystems is completely destroyed in a hail of enemy fire and nuclear explosions, the player's Mech starts the "Building Phase" after combat as if their purchases and chassis have been completely restored. You must have like a subscription to that missile launcher or whatever. Now THAT'S a dystopian future.

Rewards

Once combat is over, two piles of cards are dealt. The numberof cards in each pile should be 3 + the number of players. The piles are then passed to each player in the reverse of the order that each player died, starting with the survivor. Each player takes one card from the pile and passes the pile along. The remaining cards are shuffled into the deck. Players cannot look at the second pile of reward cards until they have chosen their card from the first pile.

If there are three players; Albert, Brenda, and Charlie, then at the end of the round, the reward pile would have 6 cards in it. If Albert survived, and Charlie died first, and Brenda died second, then Albert won the round and Brenda came in second place. So Albert starts with the reward pile, chooses one card and then gives it to Brenda. Brenda then picks a card and gives the pile to Charlie. Once Charlie is done with the pile, he shuffles it into the deck. Then they do the same thing with the second reward pile.

Once all of the rewards have been alotted, the building phase of the next rounds starts!

Declaring a Winner

The player who took the most first place rounds is the winner of Mechanized! If there is a tie for winner, the player with the FEWEST tiebreaker points is the winner. Like golf rules. (to see how tiebreaker points are awarded, read the "combat" section above)
