Pilot Classes

At the start of the game you get to choose one pilot class, which gives your character a helpful bonus. The class you select will be yours for the duration of the game; it cannot be changed mid-game. More than one player can choose the same class, but no player can choose more than one class.

New players are encouraged to pick classes that are simple and easy to play, like Mech Mechanic or Veteran, but feel free to pick the class that most interests you or fits the best with your strategy.

Soldier of Fortune

The Soldier of fortune takes bounties on the players that they kill. They get a credit bonus every time they make the attack that blows up an enemy. How much they get depends on how many players are in the game. In the event that the Soldier of Fortune's mech is destroyed and its explosion causes the destruction of another player's mech, the soldier of fortune is credited for that kill; however, if a Soldier of Fortune player's mech is destroyed by another Soldier of Fortune player, and that explosion causes another mech to blow up, the player performing the attack gets credited for both kills (or more).

Veteran Soldier

The Veteran Soldier is more practiced at using their weapons in the heat of combat. As such, they are slightly more accurate with them. The Veteran must select ONE of the following categories to specialize in. For the duration of the game, they get a +1 to accuracy rolls when using weapons of the chosen category.

  • Tier 1 weapons
  • Tier 2 weapons
  • Tier 3 weapons
  • Lasers & Polaron Cannons
  • Plasma, Particle, and Ion weapons
  • Gauss & Rail weapons
  • Autocannon, Rotary Autocannon (or Gun)
  • Missiles
  • Melee weapons

Mech Mechanic

The Mech Mechanic has the tools and skills to machine precision parts that perform better. Their mech gets an additional +5 ammo storage. If a mech mechanic gets shields, they get +5 strength.

Scavenger

The Scavenger can use their keen eye for recognizing specific valuable parts and their talent for restoring broken parts to gain information about opponents' mechs and potentially salvage weapons from fallen foes.

Whenever you occupy the hex that another mech exploded on this round, you can scavenge a weapon of your choice from that mech's weapon sheet. You may do this as many times as you like during a round, and you may do this on allied and opposing mechs alike. At the end of the round, you may pick ONE of the weapons that you scavenged and either keep it for free, or sell it. When you sell a scavenged weapon, you get 25% of the cost of the weapon or you get 7 credits, whichever is less.

Banker

The Banker knows how to make aggressive short-term investments to leverage the market so they can outspend their opponents.

At the start of every round, the Banker receives 20% interest on previously saved money (round up). Each money card that the Banker uses grants an additional +12 credits after round 1.

Bitcoin Thief

The Bitcoin Thief gets a bonus for selling stolen IDs from opponents hacked during the fighting. The Bitcoin Thief gets extra credits for each unique player hacked each round. How much they get per successful hack depends on how many players are playing. The hacker must use a hacking subsystem to do hacking.
