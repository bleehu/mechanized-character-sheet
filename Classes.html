Pilot Classes

At the start of the game you get to choose one pilot class, which gives your character a helpful bonus. The class you select will be yours for the duration of the game; it cannot be changed mid-game. More than one player can choose the same class, but no player can choose more than one class.

New players are encouraged to pick classes that are simple and easy to play, like Mech Mechanic or Veteran, but feel free to pick the class that most interests you or fits the best with your strategy.

Soldier of Fortune

The Soldier of fortune takes bounties on the players that they kill. They get a credit bonus every time they make the attack that blows up an enemey. How much they get depends on how many players are in the game. In the event that the solider of fortune's mech is destroyed and its explosion causes the destruction of another player's mech, the solider of fortune is credited for that kill.

Veteran Soldier

The Veteran Soldier is more used to using their weapons in the heat of combat. As such, they are slightly more accurate with them. The Veteran must select ONE of the following categories to specialize in. For the duration of the game, they get a +1 to accuracy rolls when using weapons of the chosen category.

  • Tier 1 weapons
  • Tier 2 weapons
  • Tier 3 weapons
  • Lasers & Polaron Cannons
  • Plasma, Particle, and Ion weapons
  • Gauss & Rail weapons
  • Autocannon, Rotary, and Chain, guns
  • Missiles
  • Melee weapons

Mech Mechanic

The Mech Mechanic has the tools and skills to machine precision parts that perform better. Their mech gets an additional +5 ammo storage. If a mech mechanic gets shields, they get +5 strength.

Scavenger

The Scavenger can use their keen eye for recognizing specific valuable parts and their tallent for restoring broken parts to gain information about opponents' mechs and potentially salvage weapons from fallen foes.

Once per battle, you can inspect the wreckage of a destroyed mech. When you do, the owner of the mech must whisper to you the exact weapons they had equipped. If you survive the round, you may purchase one of the weapons from the list of the wreckage at an up to 15 credit discount. Inspecting wreckage follows the same rules as visually inspecting mechs that haven't exploded yet. See the "Visual Inspection" section in the Combat Rules.

Banker

The Banker knows how to make aggressive short-term investments to leverage the market so they can outspend their opponenets.

At the start of every round, the Banker receives 20% interest on previously saved money (round up). Each money card that the Banker uses grants an additional +12 credits after round 1.

Hacker

The hacker gets a bonus for selling stolen IDs from opponents hacked during the fighting. The hacker gets extra credits for each unique player hacked each round. How much they get per successful hack depends on how many players are playing. The hacker must use a hacking subsystem to do hacking.
